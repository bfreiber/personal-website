Research

The Community Effect: Theory and Evidence from Live Media

Working Paper

In this paper, I propose that relative to non-live forms of media (e.g., YouTube, Instagram), the evolution of live media (e.g., Twitch, Facebook Live) encourages the proliferation and survival of smaller channels resulting in lower levels of market concentration. This follows from an argument that viewers derive value from a “community effect,” which is stronger in smaller channels, where viewers experience more personal interaction, than in larger channels. This paper provides a model that describes such behavior, and compares empirics using data from Twitch, the largest player in the live-streaming esports market today.

November 2018
